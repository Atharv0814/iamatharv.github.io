Physics Formulas

Mechanics

Kinematics

Displacement (in one dimension):
x = x0 + v0 t + ½ a t2
Velocity (in one dimension):
v = v0 + a t
v2 = v02 + 2 a (x - x0)
Average Velocity:
̅v = (x - x0) / t

Dynamics

Newton’s Second Law:
F = m a
Friction Force:
ff = μ N
Normal Force (on an inclined plane):
N = mg cos θ
Centripetal Force:
Fc = m v2 / r
Gravitational Force:
Fg = G m1 m2 / r2

Work and Energy

Work Done by a Force:
W = F ⋅ d ⋅ cos θ
Kinetic Energy:
K.E. = ½ m v2
Potential Energy (gravitational):
P.E. = mgh
Work-Energy Theorem:
Wnet = Δ K.E.
Conservation of Mechanical Energy:
Etotal = K.E. + P.E. = constant

Momentum

Linear Momentum:
p = m v
Impulse:
J = F ⋅ Δ t
J = Δ p = pf - pi
Conservation of Momentum:
ptotal, initial = ptotal, final

Rotational Motion

Angular Displacement:
θ(t) = A cos(ω t + φ)
Angular Velocity:
ω = ω0 + α t
ω2 = ω02 + 2 α (θ - θ0)
Moment of Inertia:
I = ∑ mi ri2
Torque:
τ = r F sin θ
Newton’s Second Law for Rotation:
τ = I α
Rotational Kinetic Energy:
K.E.rot = ½ I ω2

Newton's Laws

First Law (Law of Inertia):
An object remains at rest or in uniform motion unless acted upon by an external force.
Second Law:
F = m a
Third Law:
For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.

Simple Harmonic Motion (SHM)

Displacement in SHM:
x(t) = A cos(ω t + φ)
Velocity in SHM:
v(t) = -A ω sin(ω t + φ)
Acceleration in SHM:
a(t) = -A ω2 cos(ω t + φ)
Angular Frequency:
ω = √(k / m)
Period:
T = 2 π √ (m / k)
Frequency:
f = 1 / T

Gravitation

Gravitational Potential Energy:
U = -G m1 m2 / r
Gravitational Field Strength:
g = G M / r2

Work-Energy Principle in Mechanics

Work Done by a Variable Force:
W = ∫x1x2 F(x) dx
Power:
P = dW / dt = F ⋅ v

Thermodynamics

Basic Concepts

First Law of Thermodynamics:
ΔU = Q - W
Internal Energy:
U = (3/2) n R T (for a monoatomic ideal gas)
Heat Capacity:
C = dQ / dT

Ideal Gas Law

Ideal Gas Law:
PV = nRT
Molar Gas Constant:
R = 8.314 J/(mol·K)

Thermodynamic Processes

Isothermal Process:
PV = constant
Adiabatic Process:
PVγ = constant
T1 V1γ-1 = T2 V2γ-1
Polytropic Process:
PVn = constant

Heat Transfer

Heat Conduction (Fourier's Law):
Q = -k A (dT / dx)
Heat Transfer by Convection:
Q = h A (Ts - Tf)
Heat Transfer by Radiation:
Q = σ A T4 (Stefan-Boltzmann Law)

Thermodynamic Cycles

Efficiency of a Heat Engine:
η = 1 - (Tc / Th)
Carnot Cycle Efficiency:
η = 1 - (Tc / Th)
Work Done in a Carnot Cycle:
W = Qh - Qc

Entropy

Change in Entropy:
ΔS = Q / T
Entropy Change for an Ideal Gas:
ΔS = n R ln (V2 / V1) + n Cv ln (T2 / T1)

Other Thermodynamic Relations

Gibbs Free Energy:
G = H - TS
Helmholtz Free Energy:
F = U - TS
Enthalpy:

Chemical Potential:
μ = ∂G / ∂n

Magnetism

Physics Concepts

Mechanics

  • Classical Mechanics: Describes the motion of bodies under the influence of forces.
  • Newtonian Mechanics: Focuses on Newton's laws of motion.
  • Rotational Dynamics: Examines the rotational motion of objects.
  • Work-Energy Principle: Relates work done by forces to changes in kinetic energy.

Thermodynamics

  • Heat: Transfer of thermal energy between systems.
  • Entropy: Measure of disorder or randomness in a system.
  • Thermodynamic Systems: Includes isolated, closed, and open systems.

Electromagnetism

  • Electric Fields: Fields that surround electric charges and exert force on other charges.
  • Magnetic Fields: Fields produced by moving charges or magnetic materials.
  • Electromagnetic Waves: Waves that consist of oscillating electric and magnetic fields.

Optics

  • Refraction: Bending of light as it passes through different media.
  • Diffraction: Spreading of waves around obstacles.
  • Interference: Overlapping of waves that results in constructive or destructive patterns.

Modern Physics

  • Quantum Mechanics: Study of particles at the quantum level.
  • Relativity: Einstein’s theories of special and general relativity.
  • Particle Physics: Study of fundamental particles and their interactions.

Fluid Mechanics

  • Fluid Properties: Characteristics such as density, viscosity, and pressure.
  • Fluid Dynamics: Study of fluids in motion and their forces.
  • Hydrodynamics: Study of the motion of fluids and their interactions with solid boundaries.

Waves

  • Wave Types: Includes transverse and longitudinal waves.
  • Wave Interference: Constructive and destructive interference patterns.
  • Wave-Particle Duality: Concept that particles exhibit both wave and particle properties.

Astronomy

  • Celestial Mechanics: Study of the motions of celestial bodies.
  • Cosmology: Study of the origins and evolution of the universe.
  • Stellar Physics: Study of the physical properties of stars.

Major Discoveries

  • Newton's Laws of Motion: Formulated by Isaac Newton in the 17th century, laying the foundation of classical mechanics.
  • Theory of Relativity: Proposed by Albert Einstein, revolutionizing our understanding of space, time, and gravity.
  • Quantum Mechanics: Developed by Max Planck and others, explaining phenomena at atomic and subatomic levels.
  • Electromagnetic Theory: Formulated by James Clerk Maxwell, unifying electricity, magnetism, and light.

Physics Images

Important Instruments

Cathode Ray Tube: Used in early televisions and oscilloscopes.

Hubble Space Telescope: Provides high-resolution images of distant galaxies.

Particle Collider: Used to accelerate particles to high energies for collision experiments.

Microscope: Used to observe small objects that cannot be seen with the naked eye.

Spectrometer: Measures the properties of light over a specific portion of the electromagnetic spectrum.

Thermometer: Measures temperature through various methods including mercury, alcohol, and digital sensors.

Barometer: Measures atmospheric pressure, useful for weather forecasting.

Pendulum: Used to study motion and gravitational acceleration.

Galvanometer: Measures small electrical currents.

Voltmeter: Measures the electrical potential difference between two points in a circuit.

Oscilloscope: Used to view and analyze the waveform of electronic signals.

Laser: Emits light through optical amplification based on stimulated emission of radiation.

Ammeter: Measures the current flowing through a circuit.

Inductor: Stores energy in a magnetic field when electric current flows through it.

Capacitor: Stores electrical energy in an electric field for various applications.

Resistor: Limits the flow of electric current in a circuit.

Digital Multimeter: Measures voltage, current, and resistance.

Transformer: Changes the voltage of an alternating current (AC) supply.

Sensor: Detects changes in physical conditions such as temperature, light, or motion and converts them into signals.

Magnetometer: Measures the strength and direction of magnetic fields.

Cryostat: Maintains extremely low temperatures for scientific experiments.

Frequency Counter: Measures the frequency of oscillating signals.

Magnetron: Generates microwaves using a magnetic field and oscillating electrons.

X-Ray Tube: Produces X-rays for imaging and diagnostic purposes.

Atomic Force Microscope: Measures surface forces at the atomic scale with high precision.

Fluorometer: Measures the fluorescence of samples to analyze their properties.

Geiger Counter: Detects and measures radiation levels, including alpha, beta, and gamma rays.

Laser Interferometer: Measures tiny displacements using laser interference patterns.

Spectrophotometer: Measures the intensity of light at different wavelengths to analyze the absorption properties of materials.
