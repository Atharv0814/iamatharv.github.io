Physics Concepts

Mechanics

  • Classical Mechanics: Describes the motion of bodies under the influence of forces.
  • Newtonian Mechanics: Focuses on Newton's laws of motion.
  • Rotational Dynamics: Examines the rotational motion of objects.
  • Work-Energy Principle: Relates work done by forces to changes in kinetic energy.

Thermodynamics

  • Heat: Transfer of thermal energy between systems.
  • Entropy: Measure of disorder or randomness in a system.
  • Thermodynamic Systems: Includes isolated, closed, and open systems.

Electromagnetism

  • Electric Fields: Fields that surround electric charges and exert force on other charges.
  • Magnetic Fields: Fields produced by moving charges or magnetic materials.
  • Electromagnetic Waves: Waves that consist of oscillating electric and magnetic fields.

Optics

  • Refraction: Bending of light as it passes through different media.
  • Diffraction: Spreading of waves around obstacles.
  • Interference: Overlapping of waves that results in constructive or destructive patterns.

Modern Physics

  • Quantum Mechanics: Study of particles at the quantum level.
  • Relativity: Einstein’s theories of special and general relativity.
  • Particle Physics: Study of fundamental particles and their interactions.

Fluid Mechanics

  • Fluid Properties: Characteristics such as density, viscosity, and pressure.
  • Fluid Dynamics: Study of fluids in motion and their forces.
  • Hydrodynamics: Study of the motion of fluids and their interactions with solid boundaries.

Waves

  • Wave Types: Includes transverse and longitudinal waves.
  • Wave Interference: Constructive and destructive interference patterns.
  • Wave-Particle Duality: Concept that particles exhibit both wave and particle properties.

Astronomy

  • Celestial Mechanics: Study of the motions of celestial bodies.
  • Cosmology: Study of the origins and evolution of the universe.
  • Stellar Physics: Study of the physical properties of stars.

Major Discoveries

  • Newton's Laws of Motion: Formulated by Isaac Newton in the 17th century, laying the foundation of classical mechanics.
  • Theory of Relativity: Proposed by Albert Einstein, revolutionizing our understanding of space, time, and gravity.
  • Quantum Mechanics: Developed by Max Planck and others, explaining phenomena at atomic and subatomic levels.
  • Electromagnetic Theory: Formulated by James Clerk Maxwell, unifying electricity, magnetism, and light.
